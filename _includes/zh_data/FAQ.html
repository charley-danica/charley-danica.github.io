Frequently Asked Questions

What is the dress code?

The dress code is flexible and meant to be comfortable. We’ll have guests coming from many different countries and climates, so please wear what feels right to you. August in Serbia can be quite warm, and comfortable shoes for dancing are highly recommended.

What will the weather be like?

Late August in Novi Sad is typically hot and sunny. Daytime temperatures often range from 28–35°C, with evenings cooling to around 18–22°C. The ceremony will be outdoors with shade available, and dinner will be indoors with A/C.

What about food and dietary restrictions?

Please let us know about any food allergies or dietary restrictions when you RSVP. The standard menu includes fish and meat, but the restaurant has confirmed they can accommodate allergies and special diets. Traditional Serbian cuisine also includes “fasting” dishes, which are vegan.

Will there be an open bar?

Yes, drinks are on us.

Are children welcome?

Yes, kids are very welcome. Please indicate their age in the RSVP, because children under 4 will be seated with their parents.

What should international guests know about payments, electricity, and phones?

Credit and debit cards (Visa and Mastercard) are widely accepted throughout Novi Sad. The local currency is the Serbian Dinar (RSD), and ATMs are easy to find.

Serbia uses Type C and F electrical plugs (230V), which are standard across most of Europe. Guests from North America and parts of Asia may need a plug adapter.

Wi-Fi will be available at the restaurant, and international guests often find local SIM cards or eSIMs affordable and easy to use.

Should we bring a gift?

Having you with us is what matters most, especially for those making a big trip. If you’d like to give a gift, cash is preferred, but your presence is more than enough.

What about plus-ones?

Please refer to your invitation for named guests or plus-ones, but feel free to reach out to us if you have someone else you would like to bring.

Who should we contact if we have questions?

You can contact either of us directly, we’ll do our best to help.
